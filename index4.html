There is a discussion about Trevor Noah's journey in the first two chapters. The disparities and racial inequalities that existed in South Africa.
He discusses the Immorality Act of 1927, a rule passed in South Africa that made it illegal for white people and black people to have sex.These
prepared the ground for discussing racial tensions, discrimination, and apartheid's effects on people's lives and families. Noah's mother Patricia
fled her house to live and work in Johannesburg. Because she could not trust anyone and because it was against the law for Black people to reside
in the city,she was forced to hide. Being "a mixed child," Noah had to hide most of the time to avoid being seen, which had an impact on his
relationship with his father. He discusses the customs, history, and culture of his native nation. The story of Noah's voyage tells of South Africa's
fight for equality and racial injustice. Every Sunday, Trevor's mother, a devoted Christian, escorted her family to many different churches. African Americans
in South Africa were classified into tribes before the implementation of apartheid. There was a lengthy history of hostilities between these tribes and the
Xhosa and Zulu. Because Xhosa women were viewed as prostitutes, a bus driver once chastised Noah's mother for taking their kids on a late-night trip without
a male companion.Tribalism is a major problem in Busia County since one tribe is given preference. While some tribes are left behind, others enjoy easier
access to resources and jobs.These play a major role in the existence of the rich and poor classes. Most of the time, single mothers are looked down upon
and criticized in society for not having a man. Women are religious in society. You'll discover that ladies attend meetings, women's day, and fellowships in
addition to Sunday church services. They ensure their kids attend church and serve as the driver of Christianity in their homes. They relocate to different
churches most of the time. They think that every church has something unique to offer. Since many people do not have a source of income and rebel against
stealing, insecurity is a daily issue that needs to be handled. Drivers for Uber and Matatu may assault their passengers and commit crimes. The matatu
drivers argue over patrons and call one other names offensively.
